The Chimera

Origin: Greek

The Chimera was a monstrous fire-breathing hybrid creature of Lycia in Asia Minor. It was composed of the parts of more than one animal: usually depicted as a lion, with the head of a goat arising from its back, and a tail that might end with a snake's head. The Chimera was eventually slain by the hero Bellerophon riding the winged horse Pegasus.

Weakness: Being pierced by a spear tipped with lead, which melted from the Chimera's fiery breath and suffocated it.
