Fenrir

Origin: Norse Mythology

A monstrous wolf who is the son of Loki. He is destined to kill Odin during the events of Ragnarök, but will be slain by Odin's son Víðarr in turn. The gods attempted to bind Fenrir with various chains, but he broke free each time. Eventually, they tricked him into being bound with a magical ribbon called Gleipnir, which he could not break. However, Fenrir's mouth was left unbound, and he bit off the hand of the god Tyr when he realized he had been tricked.

Weakness: The magical fetter Gleipnir, which was made from six impossible things: the sound of a cat's footsteps, the beard of a woman, the roots of a mountain, the sinews of a bear, the breath of a fish, and the spittle of a bird.
