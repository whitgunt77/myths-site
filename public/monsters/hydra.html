The Hydra

Origin: Greek

The Lernaean Hydra was a serpentine water monster in Greek and Roman mythology. It possessed many heads, and for each head cut off, two more would grow back in its place. The Hydra was eventually slain by the hero Heracles as one of his Twelve Labors.

Weakness: Cauterizing the neck stumps with fire prevented the heads from regenerating.
