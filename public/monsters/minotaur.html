The Minotaur

Origin: Greek Mythology

A creature with the head of a bull and the body of a man, the Minotaur was born from the union of Pasiphaë, the wife of King Minos of Crete, and a majestic bull sent by the god Poseidon. The Minotaur was kept in a labyrinth designed by the architect Daedalus, where it was fed with human sacrifices until it was slain by the hero Theseus.
