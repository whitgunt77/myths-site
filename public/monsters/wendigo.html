Wendigo

Origin: Algonquian Mythology

A malevolent, cannibalistic spirit associated with famine, greed, and winter. They are often described as thin, skeletal, and posessing an insatiable hunger for human flesh. The Wendigo is said to possess humans, driving them to madness and cannibalism. It is a symbol of the dangers of isolation and the consequences of giving in to base desires.

Weakness: Fire, or being pierced through the heart with silver or steel.
